Here's what he said: It's explained in this comment: https://github.com/GenerationSoftware/pt-v5-vault/blob/b1deb5d494c25f885c34c83f014c8a855c5e2749/src/Vault.sol#L548 Basically, we deposit into the YieldVault any amount of underlying assets that may be currently held by the Vault. This way when we _mint(), we ensure that the exchange rate is accurate. Since the Liquidator is only aware of the _amountIn of Prize Tokens contributed: https://github.com/GenerationSoftware/pt-v5-liquidator/blob/94230890139bc12d3f5c8a9cc0838a5c71a221d2/src/LiquidationRouter.sol#L70 An attacker may try to manipulate the exchange rate by sending underlying assets directly to the Vault to inflate the amount of Vault shares received, that's why we have the following condition: _amountOut >= _vaultAssets We should not mint more Vault shares than Prize Tokens contributed. The totalSupply and exchange rate will be updated once we _mint(): https://github.com/GenerationSoftware/pt-v5-vault/blob/b1deb5d494c25f885c34c83f014c8a855c5e2749/src/Vault.sol#L1123 So we don't want to deposit into the Yield Vault more underlying assets than the _amountOut of Vault shares minted since the exchange rate is the total amount of underlying assets withdrawable from the Yield Vault over the total supply of Vault shares: https://github.com/GenerationSoftware/pt-v5-vault/blob/b1deb5d494c25f885c34c83f014c8a855c5e2749/src/Vault.sol#L1183 If we deposit more underlying assets than Vault shares minted, we would inflate the exchange rate.
